Analyzing how Bitcoin Fear & Greed sentiment relates to trader behavior and performance on Hyperliquid.

## 🔧 Feature Engineering (cleaning.ipynb)

The cleaning notebook engineers these metrics at **daily level per account**:

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| `daily_pnl` | Total closed PnL per account per day |
| `trade_count` | Number of trades per account per day |
| `avg_trade_size` | Average position size in USD (leverage proxy) |
| `win_rate` | % of trades with positive PnL |
| `buy_sell_ratio` | Buy trades ÷ Sell trades (long/short bias proxy) |
| `sentiment_value` | Fear & Greed score for that day (0–100) |
| `sentiment_class` | Regime: Extreme Fear / Fear / Neutral / Greed / Extreme Greed |

## 📝 Summary

**Methodology:** Two datasets were merged — Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index (daily sentiment scores) 
and Hyperliquid trade records (32 traders, May 2023–May 2025). Trades were aggregated to daily 
level per account to engineer 7 metrics: daily PnL, trade count, avg trade size, win rate, 
buy/sell ratio, sentiment value and class.

**Key Insights:**
1. Fear days generate 60% higher average PnL than Greed days ($5,329 vs $3,318)
2. Greed days have the worst single-day drawdown (-$358,963) — highest tail risk
3. Traders place 1.76x more trades during Extreme Fear than Extreme Greed
4. High frequency traders earn 49% more than low frequency traders ($383K vs $257K)
5. No single metric predicts PnL — win rate, trade count and size all have weak correlations

**Strategy Recommendations:**
- During Fear: trade more actively and place bigger bets — data shows these are the best days
- During Greed: trade smaller, set tighter stops, take profits early — worst risk/reward regime
